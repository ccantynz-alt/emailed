# The Vision — Why We Exist

## The Problem

Big tech has rigged the game. They build tools that businesses and
people depend on, then raise prices, lock users in, and make it
harder to leave. The average person and small business owner is
grinding harder than ever, paying more than ever, and getting
squeezed from every direction:

- Google Workspace: keeps raising prices, removes features from
  free tiers, forces upgrades
- Microsoft 365: complex licensing, expensive per-seat costs,
  features locked behind enterprise tiers
- AWS/Cloud providers: bills that grow unpredictably, intentionally
  complex pricing to prevent comparison shopping
- Stripe: takes 2.9% + 30c of every transaction — on a $10M/year
  business that's $290,000+ going to payment processing
- Twilio: charges per SMS, per minute, per everything — costs
  scale faster than revenue
- Auth0/Clerk: charges per user — punishes you for growing

These companies were built to extract maximum value from users,
not to deliver maximum value TO users.

## The Mission

**Build an ecosystem that makes life easier and more affordable
for the average person and small business.**

Not easier for enterprises with 10,000 employees and unlimited
budgets. Easier for:
- The solo founder bootstrapping their first product
- The small business owner who can't afford a $500/month tech stack
- The family business that needs email, a website, and payments
  without hiring an IT department
- The developer who wants powerful tools without enterprise pricing
- The average person who deserves privacy, security, and good
  technology without being the product

## The Principles

### 1. Simple Over Complex
If a feature requires a manual to understand, we failed. Every
product should be usable by someone with no technical background.
AI handles the complexity so humans don't have to.

### 2. Affordable Over Extractive
We will never price gouge. We will never lock essential features
behind enterprise tiers. We will never punish users for growing.
Fair pricing that scales with value, not with vendor leverage.

### 3. Transparent Over Opaque
No surprise bills. No hidden fees. No intentionally confusing
pricing pages. Users should know exactly what they're paying and
why, every single month.

### 4. Empowering Over Trapping
Users stay because the technology is genuinely valuable, not
because we made it hard to leave. Full data export, standard
protocols, no proprietary lock-in tricks. If we lose a customer,
it's because we failed to deliver value — and that's on us.

### 5. AI Works For Users, Not Against Them
AI should make life easier, not harvest data for advertising or
manipulate behavior. Our AI exists to save users time, protect
them from threats, and handle complexity they shouldn't have to
deal with. Their data is theirs. Period.

### 6. Built To Last, Not To Flip
This is not a startup built to be acquired. This is a legacy
built to be handed down. Every decision should be made with
the question: "Will this still be the right choice in 15 years?"

## The Competitive Advantage

No single company has ever built all of this with a shared AI
brain from day one. Amazon came closest but their products are
siloed. Google has the AI but they're an advertising company
that sells user data. Microsoft has the enterprise market but
their products are bloated and expensive.

We are building in an era where AI can replace the 150-person
teams these companies needed. One small team with AI can build
what took them thousands of engineers and decades. They can't
start over. We can start right.

The big guys pushed the little guys around because they had
scale and we didn't have the tools to compete. AI changed that.
Now the playing field is level, and we're going to build
something they can't touch.

## The Promise

To every person who uses our products: we will never stop
making your life easier. We will never raise prices because we
can. We will never sell your data. We will never make you the
product. You are the customer, and you deserve technology that
works for you.

This is not just a business. This is a legacy.
